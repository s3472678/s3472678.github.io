Project Idea

Overview

Invaice.io is an all in one, project management, CRM and invoicing and payments product focused on the digital services market, aimed at freelance and self-employed businesses. The Invaice.io product provides its customers with a way to not only invoice their customers for work and services delivered, but also follow up with customers and request payments that have passed their due date through AI enhanced features that can communicate with customers on the users behalf. Invaice.io aims to automate all the boring parts of owning a small business - empowering users to focus on delivering and growing their business.

Motivation

The percentage of freelancers and gig-economy workers that make up the workforce has increased since 2014, with freelancing potentially becoming the majority of how people are employed in the workforce (Upwork, 2017). While software providing support for invoicing and payments is not a new concept, the software vendors of these products often target large to enterprise level companies for their deals, as the features they require are usually complex and are able to charge higher subscription fees (MYOB, 2023). Where larger vendors focus on enterprise level features, Invaice.io intends to focus solely on servicing the freelance and self-employed sector, by creating tools that fit their unique use cases.

Description

Invaice.io aims to be a one-stop-shop for freelancers and small business owners, with a mission of empowering them to focus on the things they do best, not spending time chasing invoices. Invaice.io does this through comprehensive features that provide users with their own CRM, Project Management Tool and Payments facilitation.

Customers

Users are able to create and import lists of their Customers into the product, providing them with their own Customer Relationship Management (CRM) tool. Users will be able to see a holistic view of each of their Customers, how much they have spent, previous projects and communications between the two parties.

Projects

As freelancers tend to work on multiple projects (Fountain, 2022) providing a way for users to plan, track and schedule projects is essential. Invaice.io provides a project management feature set including kanban boards and project schedulers that enables freelancers to project manage their work, charge for their work and be paid for their work in one tool.

Invoices

Using Invaice.io’s invoice template builder, users are able to build their own invoice template or select from pre-made designs. Additionally, users are able to customise what their customers see on their invoices, with the ability to link line items back to items on their project board, or links to completed work artefacts. Finally, users are able to send their invoices to customers through multiple channels, including email, online chat, or sms.

Payments

Getting paid is potentially the most important part of a freelancers business. Invaice.io allows users to accept payments from credit cards and bank transfers without having to enter into an agreement with a bank by partnering with payments infrastructure providers such as Stripe (Stripe, 2023). Providing users with a way to accept payments inside Invaice.io instead of simply providing their banking details on the invoice itself provides a more seamless experience for their customers, with customers able to make payment online - directly through their invoice link which we believe will help users to have their invoices paid on time.

Enhanced Communications

In addition to the above features that focus on empowering the freelance and self-employed experience with an all in one product for their project management, invoicing and payments - Invaice.io also provides its users with the ability to auto generate communications with their customers. Powered by a large-language model (LLM), Invaice.io users will be able to select pre-generated text to add to correspondence with their customers, whether it be asking customers to review a design, pay an invoice or follow up on a past-due invoice - users will be able to spend less time communicating back and forth with their customers and more time on their business.

Tools and Techniques

In order to support this project, the only hardware required will be a computer with an internet connection. Software required will include common software development tools such as a code editor such as VsCode (VisualStudio, 2023).

There will be multiple software components required to build this project. First of all, the project will require a website for marketing purposes, so that potential users can find and learn more about the product and features that Invaice.io offers. In addition the marketing site, Invaice.io will require the product itself to be written.

There are multiple methodologies and technologies that can be used to build a web application product, at the very least Invaice.io will require both a front-end project and a back-end project.

The front end will house all of the logic and user interfaces where the user will primarily be interacting with the product. There are multiple front-end software frameworks available that provide developers with a rapid way to build their product (Monocubed, 2021).

The back-end project will house all of the business logic to power the features that users will interact with through the front-end, additionally the back-end project will house all integrations to 3rd party services that Invaice.io will leverage in order to provide the functionality required. Integrations such as Stripe for processing payments, and OpenAI for enhanced communications. Utilising 3rd party services allows Invaice.io to leverage the expertise and knowledge from other companies in its own service offering. Much like the front-end, the back-end also will leverage existing frameworks to accelerate development (Back4App, 2023).

Skills Required

UI design and software development skills are required to build this project. UI designers will need to be able to design seamless experiences that align with the branding and values of the Invaice.io project with its focus on simplifying the freelancer experience.

Software developers will be required to have an understanding of both front and back end technologies, so “Full Stack Developers” (Freecodecamp, 2021) that can write software on both UI and backend business logic are required.

There are currently around 2 million IT professionals in Australia (Herohunt.ai, 2023), and the roles, skills and technologies listed above are common across active listings for both Full Stack developers and UI Designers (Seek, 2023).

Outcome

Once completed and released to the market, Invaice.io will have a tangible impact on the day to day lives of self-employed and freelancers - by providing them with an all in one toolset, designed to simplify the running of their business. With Invaice.io available, this should influence more people to turn to freelancing, enriching their professional lives as they have the freedom to be their own boss, choose when and what they work on (Upwork, 2017).
